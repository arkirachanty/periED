Tampons

Tampons are like tiny, soft, cotton rockets inserted in your vagina to absorb your menstrual flow before it leaves the body. Yes, it might feel a little uncomfortable if you insert it incorrectly, (don’t worry, you’ll be able to figure it out with some practice!) and no- tampons will not make you lose your virginity.

Although tampons may take a while to get used to, the process is so worth it. Let’s be real- you love swimming in lakes, and pads just don’t cut it. Plus, they can feel kind of like diapers and worrying whether they can be seen through your LuluLemons is unnecessary paranoia no one has time for.

So knucks to modern science and technology because T A M P O N S. They’re sold at your local pharmacy/ grocery store in a bunch of different sizes and absorbencies to match what you need. They come in cute packaging and colors so that you’ll start looking forward to your next period. (Okay, that’s an exaggeration. But really. Tampons are great!) The best part is that you won’t even feel an overwhelming rush every time you stand up because the cotton absorbs your blood before it exits your vagina. (Yeah cotton rocket, you go.) You can continue with your daily activities as if Aunt Flow isn’t there- and yes, that includes swimming!


Pads

Pads are the OG of feminine products. They’ve come a long way since the ancient Chinese method of stuffing sand into a handkerchief to store up blood (True story!) Today’s pads are comfortable, easy to use, and reliable. They are safe (no risk of TSS) and easy to use and change- unlike tampons, you won’t get blood on your hands and if you’re hemophobic, you can change pads without looking.

In short, pads are rectangular cotton material that you stick to your underwear to soak up blood after blood leaves your vagina. Stickers. Pads are basically big cotton stickers. And tbh who doesn’t like stickers? Many modern day pads have additional “wings” on the side, extra material that you fold over the sides of your underwear to further prevent leaking. Like tampons, they come in all absorbencies and sizes (by pad sizes we mean different lengths) that you can find at your local pharmacy/grocery store. Bonus: Many pads today are papery thin (you can barely tell they’re there), extremely absorbent and reliable.


PantyLiners

You can think of panty liners as mini pads used when pads are considered too “excessive”. Panty-liners generally replace pads near the end of your period, when you want a layer beneath tampon as added protection against leaking, or just when you have slight vaginal discharge (don’t worry, this is normal!) They look like pads but are thinner and narrower in construction.


Cups

Okay, now we’re talking unconventional methods. Menstrual cups are the least common form of period protection because adults generally fail to publicize this product. Menstrual cups are way underrated tbh. They’re environmentally friendly and comfortable with practice. Menstrual cups are flexible bell-shaped collectors that gather all of those lady liquids before they exit the body, thus providing similar benefits as tampons. They also have an added advantage of environmental friendliness and fewer health risks such as TSS and vaginal infections that come with repeated tampon usage. Menstrual cups are reusable and are emptied then washed before reinsertion.

Because using menstrual cups generally require a certain level of skill, they are best used after you have had some experience with using tampons (insertion methods can be difficult when you first start your period!)
